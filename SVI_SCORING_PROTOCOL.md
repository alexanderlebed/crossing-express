# Sovereign Volatility Index (SVI) — Scoring Protocol v1.0

SVI is a structured expert-model index designed to evaluate jurisdictional volatility across mobility-relevant vectors.

Each vector is assessed across three dimensions:

- F — Frequency of Change (0–10)
- S — Severity of Impact (0–10)
- P — Predictability (0–10)

Vector Score Formula:

Vector Score = (F + S + (10 - P)) / 3 × 10

Final SVI Formula:

SVI =
RA × 0.25 +
MPS × 0.20 +
CTE × 0.20 +
TRF × 0.15 +
GDR × 0.20


---

## 1. Regulatory Acceleration (RA)

Frequency (F):
0–2: Major legislative changes once every 2–3 years  
3–5: Annual regulatory changes  
6–8: Multiple reforms per year  
9–10: Continuous reform cycle

Severity (S):
0–3: Minor administrative adjustments  
4–6: Noticeable compliance impact  
7–8: Structural impact on residency/tax/capital  
9–10: Systemic overhaul

Predictability (P):
0–3: Highly unpredictable  
4–6: Partially predictable  
7–10: Structured policy roadmap


---

## 2. Migration Policy Stability (MPS)

Evaluate changes in residency pathways, visa regimes and citizenship access.

Apply same F / S / P logic.


---

## 3. Capital Transparency Exposure (CTE)

Evaluate:
- Reporting intensity
- Cross-border disclosure obligations
- Banking compliance layers


---

## 4. Tax Regime Fluidity (TRF)

Assess:
- Frequency of tax reform
- Structural changes in income taxation
- Stability of incentives


---

## 5. Geopolitical Discontinuity Risk (GDR)

Evaluate:
- Sanctions exposure
- Diplomatic instability
- Regional fragmentation risk


---

SVI is a qualitative structured expert index.
It is not predictive and not data-automated.
