# Sovereign Volatility Index (SVI)
## Scoring Protocol v1.1 — Operational Framework

---

## 1. Purpose

The Sovereign Volatility Index (SVI) is a structured expert-model index designed to evaluate jurisdictional volatility across mobility-relevant vectors.

SVI is:

- Non-predictive
- Scenario-aware
- Expert-structured
- Non-automated
- Analytical compression instrument

It is not investment advice and not legal advisory output.

---

## 2. Structural Logic

SVI evaluates five core volatility vectors:

1. Regulatory Acceleration (RA)
2. Migration Policy Stability (MPS)
3. Capital Transparency Exposure (CTE)
4. Tax Regime Fluidity (TRF)
5. Geopolitical Discontinuity Risk (GDR)

Each vector is assessed across three scoring dimensions:

- F — Frequency of Change (0–10)
- S — Severity of Impact (0–10)
- P — Predictability (0–10)

---

## 3. Vector Score Formula

Each vector is calculated as:

Vector Score = ((F + S + (10 - P)) / 3) × 10

Interpretation:

- Higher Frequency → higher volatility
- Higher Severity → higher volatility
- Lower Predictability → higher volatility

Vector score range: 0–100

---

## 4. Final SVI Formula

The weighted index is calculated as:

SVI =  
(RA × 0.25) +  
(MPS × 0.20) +  
(CTE × 0.20) +  
(TRF × 0.15) +  
(GDR × 0.20)

---

## 5. Weighting Rationale

| Vector | Weight | Rationale |
|--------|--------|-----------|
| RA | 25% | Regulatory tempo influences all other exposures |
| MPS | 20% | Direct impact on residency architecture |
| CTE | 20% | Critical for cross-border capital positioning |
| TRF | 15% | Important but often downstream effect |
| GDR | 20% | Non-linear systemic discontinuity factor |

---

## 6. Vector Definitions

### 6.1 Regulatory Acceleration (RA)

Measures legislative tempo and structural reform cycles.

Frequency (F):
0–2 → Rare major reform  
3–5 → Annual reform cycle  
6–8 → Multiple reform waves per year  
9–10 → Continuous regulatory mutation  

Severity (S):
0–3 → Administrative refinement  
4–6 → Compliance burden increase  
7–8 → Structural system redesign  
9–10 → Systemic framework overhaul  

Predictability (P):
0–3 → Reactive governance  
4–6 → Partial roadmap structure  
7–10 → Structured multi-year policy planning  

---

### 6.2 Migration Policy Stability (MPS)

Evaluates:

- Residency pathway durability
- Visa regime volatility
- Citizenship structural access

Uses same F / S / P structure.

---

### 6.3 Capital Transparency Exposure (CTE)

Evaluates:

- Reporting intensity (CRS/FATCA)
- Cross-border disclosure obligations
- Banking compliance layers
- Financial surveillance expansion

Uses same F / S / P structure.

---

### 6.4 Tax Regime Fluidity (TRF)

Evaluates:

- Frequency of tax reform
- Structural income taxation changes
- Stability of tax incentives
- Retroactive legislative adjustments

Uses same F / S / P structure.

---

### 6.5 Geopolitical Discontinuity Risk (GDR)

Evaluates:

- Sanctions exposure
- Diplomatic instability
- Regional fragmentation risk
- Strategic bloc realignment

Uses same F / S / P structure.

---
