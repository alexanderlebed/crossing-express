About Crossing

Crossing was created to introduce structure into one of the most complex personal decisions a person can make: jurisdictional relocation.

Traditional relocation advice is fragmented — legal opinions, tax notes, visa instructions — but rarely modeled as an integrated sovereign decision framework.

Why SRIM Exists

Sovereign Relocation Intelligence Modeling (SRIM) was developed to evaluate cross-border decisions through structured comparative modeling rather than opinion-based guidance.

Relocation is not simply about moving. It is about legal status, tax exposure, economic sustainability, political stability and long-term optionality.

Positioning

Crossing does not replace lawyers or tax professionals. It structures decision space before legal execution.

The framework is designed for individuals making serious cross-border life decisions and for future integration into professional advisory environments.

Founder

The SRIM framework was initiated by Alexander Lebed as an independent conceptual infrastructure project focused on sovereign mobility and jurisdictional intelligence.
