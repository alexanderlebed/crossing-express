Sovereign Volatility Index (SVI)

The Sovereign Volatility Index (SVI) is a composite analytical metric designed to measure jurisdictional instability across mobility-relevant structural vectors.

Core Vectors

Regulatory Acceleration
Measures frequency and unpredictability of legislative and compliance changes affecting individuals and capital.
Migration Policy Stability
Evaluates volatility of residency pathways, visa regimes and citizenship frameworks.
Capital Transparency Exposure
Assesses reporting intensity, cross-border financial disclosure and systemic compliance layers.
Tax Regime Fluidity
Tracks structural changes in taxation models impacting international income holders.
Geopolitical Discontinuity Risk
Models exposure to sanctions, diplomatic fractures and structural realignment events.
SVI is not a predictive instrument. It is a structured volatility mapping tool within the SRIM analytical framework.
