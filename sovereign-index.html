Sovereign Volatility Index (SVI)

The Sovereign Volatility Index (SVI) is a composite analytical metric designed to measure jurisdictional instability across mobility-relevant structural vectors. It reflects the acceleration of regulatory change, migration policy volatility and capital exposure dynamics.

Core Structural Vectors

Regulatory Acceleration
Frequency and unpredictability of legislative and compliance changes.
Migration Policy Stability
Volatility of residency pathways and citizenship frameworks.
Capital Transparency Exposure
Intensity of reporting regimes and cross-border disclosure requirements.
Tax Regime Fluidity
Structural changes in taxation affecting international income holders.
Geopolitical Discontinuity Risk
Exposure to sanctions, diplomatic fractures and systemic realignments.
SVI is not predictive. It is a structured volatility mapping instrument within the SRIM framework. Updated quarterly.
