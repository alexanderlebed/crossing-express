SRIM Core Vectors

The Sovereign Risk Intelligence Model (SRIM) evaluates jurisdictional stability through five structural vectors relevant to cross-border relocation decisions. These vectors measure institutional pressure, accessibility of migration frameworks, residency pathways, geopolitical exposure and societal security conditions.

Tax Pressure

Measures the fiscal burden placed on internationally mobile individuals.

  • Income taxation
  • Capital gains taxation
  • Wealth taxes
  • Reporting obligations
  • Enforcement intensity
1 very low → 5 extreme

Migration Accessibility

Measures the practical accessibility of entry and legal stay for foreign residents.

  • Visa availability
  • Administrative barriers
  • Entry requirements
  • Language requirements
  • Bureaucratic complexity
1 very accessible → 5 restricted

Residency Path

Evaluates the stability and clarity of legal pathways toward long-term residence.

  • Residence permit availability
  • Renewability
  • Permanent residency path
  • Citizenship accessibility
  • Administrative predictability
1 fast and clear → 5 blocked

Geopolitical Risk

Measures sovereign-level instability that may affect long-term residence.

  • War exposure
  • Sanctions risk
  • Political instability
  • Regulatory shocks
  • Institutional fragility
1 stable → 5 systemic risk

Public Safety

Measures societal security and crime exposure within the jurisdiction.

  • Violent crime levels
  • Organized crime presence
  • Civil unrest
  • Policing effectiveness
  • General safety conditions
1 very safe → 5 dangerous
